Why computers are stupid

A computer, unlike a human, can only be instructed in a precise grammar.
Humans can understand that the following setnances:

"I have been here all afternoon"
"I been here all day"
"Here is where I have been, throughout the day."
All mean the same thing. A computer would not understand the second statement.
Computers are also stupid that they cannot understand context or emphasis.
"How could she do it?"
"How could she do it?"
"How could she do it?"
These sentances will be understood differently by humans.
To a stupid computer, they are all the same.

Qualities of Tags

There are Void tags, and paried tags
Though the name is confusing, Void tags do not mean that they are tags that do nothing. Rather, it means tags that do not require a closing statement.
They are "void" in the sense that there is no opening or closing.
Examples of void tags are the img and br tags.
Paired tags are tags that always appear in pairs: An open tag and close tag. Information is placed in between them.
E.g. Here is a little text
Examples of self closing tags are a, b, em, and html.

There are inline tags and there are block tags.
Inline tags make a change in the lines of text.
Block tags will create an invisible box around the text.
This box can have additional qualities added to it.
Examples of inline are b, em, and span. Examples of block are p, blockquote and div.

Div and Span

Div and Span are paired tags.
They are generic tags that operated as block or inline respectively.
Each tag does nothing until given additional instructions via CSS.
